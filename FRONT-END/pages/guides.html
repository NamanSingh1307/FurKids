📚 Pet Care Guides

🍗 How to Feed Your Dog

Dogs are omnivores and need a balanced diet of proteins, carbohydrates, fats, vitamins, and minerals.

  • Puppies (under 1 year): 3–4 meals per day
  • Adult dogs (1–7 years): 2 meals per day
  • Senior dogs (7+ years): 2 smaller meals per day
  • Always provide fresh water throughout the day
  • Measure portions based on body weight — check the food packaging
⚠️ Never feed: onions, garlic, grapes, raisins, chocolate, xylitol (sugar-free sweetener), avocado, or cooked bones.
✅ Good foods: boiled chicken, plain rice, carrots, sweet potato, and commercial vet-approved dog food.

🐱 How to Feed Your Cat

Cats are obligate carnivores — they must have meat in their diet to survive. Unlike dogs, they cannot synthesise certain nutrients from plant sources.

  • Feed twice a day — morning and evening
  • Kittens need 3–4 small meals per day
  • Provide both wet food (for hydration) and dry kibble
  • Avoid free-feeding dry food as it can lead to obesity
  • Fresh water must always be available
⚠️ Never feed cats: raw fish (regularly), onions, garlic, alcohol, grapes, or dog food (it lacks taurine).

🎓 Basic Dog Training Tips

Dogs learn best through positive reinforcement. Be patient, consistent, and always end sessions on a positive note.

  • Start early — puppies as young as 7–8 weeks can learn basic commands
  • Short sessions — 5–10 minutes, 2–3 times daily is more effective than one long session
  • Use high-value treats — small pieces of chicken or cheese work well as rewards
  • Consistency — every family member should use the same commands
  • Never punish — redirect unwanted behaviour; ignore and reward the good
✅ Essential commands to teach first: Sit, Stay, Come, Leave it, Down. Master these before advanced tricks.

🏠 House Training / Potty Training

  • Take the puppy outside every 2 hours, and immediately after eating or waking up
  • Choose a specific outdoor spot — the scent helps the puppy understand
  • Praise and reward immediately after they go outside
  • Accidents indoors: clean with enzymatic cleaner (not bleach) to remove scent
  • Expect full training to take 4–6 months for young puppies

✂️ Dog Grooming Basics

  • Bathing: Every 4–8 weeks depending on breed and activity level. Use dog-specific shampoo only.
  • Brushing: Short coats — weekly. Medium coats — 2–3 times/week. Long coats — daily to prevent matting.
  • Nail trimming: Every 3–4 weeks. Trim just the tip — avoid the pink 'quick' which contains blood vessels.
  • Ear cleaning: Check weekly for redness, smell, or discharge. Use vet-approved ear cleaner.
  • Teeth brushing: 2–3 times per week with a dog-specific toothpaste (never human toothpaste).
✅ Start grooming routines early so your pet becomes comfortable with handling.

🐱 Cat Grooming Basics

  • Most cats self-groom — but short-haired cats benefit from weekly brushing
  • Long-haired cats (Persian, Maine Coon) need daily brushing to avoid tangles
  • Cats rarely need bathing — only when they get into something messy
  • Trim nails every 2–3 weeks; provide a scratching post to prevent furniture damage
  • Check ears monthly for dark debris (sign of ear mites)

🩺 Vaccination Schedule (India)

  • 6–8 weeks: Distemper, Parvovirus, Hepatitis (DHPPi)
  • 10–12 weeks: DHPPi booster + Leptospirosis
  • 14–16 weeks: DHPPi + Rabies (mandatory by law)
  • Annual boosters: All core vaccines repeated every year
  • Cats: FVRCP (3-in-1) at 8, 12, 16 weeks, then annually
✅ Keep a vaccination booklet and upload photos of it to FurKids Records so you never lose track.

🪱 Deworming Schedule

  • Puppies/kittens: every 2 weeks from 2–8 weeks of age, then monthly until 6 months
  • Adults: every 3 months is the standard recommendation in India
  • Use vet-prescribed dewormers; do not use human medicines
  • Common worms in India: roundworm, hookworm, tapeworm

🐣 Bringing Home a Puppy

  • Puppy-proof your home: remove electrical cords, toxic plants, small objects
  • Set up a comfortable crate or bed in a quiet corner
  • Keep the first few days calm — avoid too many visitors
  • Begin socialisation at 3–14 weeks (critical window): expose to sounds, people, other dogs
  • Schedule a vet visit within the first week for a full health check
  • Do not let the puppy outside until fully vaccinated
✅ Puppies sleep 16–18 hours a day — this is completely normal!

🐈 Kitten First Weeks

  • Kittens under 4 weeks need feeding every 2 hours — use kitten milk replacer (not cow's milk)
  • Provide a litter box from 3 weeks — kittens instinctively use it
  • Begin handling and playing gently from 2–3 weeks to build sociability
  • Keep them warm — kittens cannot regulate body temperature until 5–6 weeks
  • Introduce solid food at 4–5 weeks (softened kitten kibble)
